Moving out is exciting - but it comes with responsibilities

home_pin

Think of renting like a money commitment, not just a room

When you move into a rental, you are not just paying weekly rent. You may also need bond, utility connections, furniture, internet, moving costs, cleaning supplies, and an emergency buffer.

favorite

This guide is for first-time renters

If you are 18–22 and moving out for study, work, independence, or safety, the goal is not to scare you. It is to help you feel prepared before signing anything.

Q1: What should I check before signing a rental agreement?

description

Read the rental agreement carefully

Check the rent amount, payment frequency, start date, length of the agreement, included appliances, and any special terms. Do not rush just because you feel pressured to secure the place.

paid

Calculate the real move-in cost

Rent is only one part. You may need bond, rent in advance, moving costs, furniture, kitchen items, internet setup, and utility connections before you even settle in.

location_on

Check the location around your lifestyle

Think about transport at night, distance from uni or work, grocery access, parking, street lighting, and whether the area feels safe when you come home late.

groups

If sharing, agree on house rules early

If you are moving in with friends or housemates, discuss rent splits, cleaning, guests, bills, food sharing, and what happens if someone wants to move out.

Q2: What is bond and why does it matter?

lock

Bond is security money

Bond is money paid at the start of a rental. It is usually held as security in case there is unpaid rent, damage, missing items, or cleaning issues at the end of the tenancy.

receipt_long

Keep proof of everything

Save your bond receipt, payment confirmations, emails, photos, condition report, and any messages with the rental provider or agent. These can protect you if there is a dispute later.

photo_camera

Your photos can protect your bond

Take clear photos and videos before moving furniture in. Focus on marks, stains, broken fittings, cracks, mould, scratches, and anything that already looks damaged.

task_alt

Normal wear and tear is different from damage

You should not lose bond for normal everyday use of the property. But damage, missing items, unpaid rent, or extra cleaning may create bond issues.

Q3: Why is the condition report so important?

fact_check

It records the condition before you move in

The condition report is evidence of what the property looked like at the start. If something is already broken, stained, or damaged, write it down.

edit_note

Do not just sign it without checking

Walk through every room and compare the report with what you actually see. Add comments where needed and attach dated photos if possible.

schedule

Act quickly after moving in

In Victoria, if you are not given a condition report before moving in, you can complete your own and give it to the rental provider or agent within 5 business days of moving in.

shield

It helps if there is a bond dispute

A detailed condition report can help show that damage or cleaning issues existed before you moved in, rather than being caused by you.

Q4: What should I know about utilities and bills?

bolt

Know what is included

Before moving in, ask whether electricity, gas, water, internet, and other services are included in the rent or paid separately.

wifi

Plan connection dates early

Internet and utilities may take time to connect. Set them up before move-in day if you need Wi-Fi for study, work, or safety.

calculate

If sharing, split bills clearly

Decide how bills will be split, whose name will be on each account, when payments are due, and how everyone will prove they have paid.

water_drop

Watch water and usage costs

Some costs may depend on the property and agreement. Ask what you are responsible for before you sign so there are no surprises.

Q5: What should I remember about rent?

calendar_month

Know exactly when rent is due

Check whether rent is weekly, fortnightly, or monthly. Set a reminder before the due date so rent does not get lost between other expenses.

account_balance_wallet

Keep rent separate from spending money

If possible, move rent into a separate account as soon as you are paid. This helps stop rent money being accidentally spent on food, transport, or going out.

trending_up

Rent can increase, but there are rules

Victorian renters can check official information about rent increases and can ask Consumer Affairs Victoria for a rent assessment if they believe an increase is above the market range.

emergency_home

Have a backup plan

Try to keep a small emergency buffer for unexpected rent, repairs, replacement keys, transport, or urgent household costs.

ABS Data Sources

The numbers and benchmarks used across this website are based on datasets from the Australian Bureau of Statistics, commonly known as the ABS.

The ABS is Australia's national statistical agency. It collects and publishes official data about population, housing, work, income, cost of living, and other areas that help explain how people live across Australia.

In Hermap, ABS data is used in context to support comparisons around living costs, income, location, and financial readiness. These figures are intended to help users better understand possible trade-offs, not to provide personal financial advice.
