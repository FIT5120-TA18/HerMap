Credit & BNPL 101

Understand credit cards, minimum repayments, interest, and buy-now-pay-later risks.

Explore

Understanding credit cards and buy-now-pay-later services is essential to avoiding hidden debt traps. Here's what you need to know.

credit_card How Credit Cards Work

A credit card lets you spend money you do not yet have, up to a set limit. If you pay the full balance each month before the due date, you usually pay no interest.

If you pay only the minimum repayment, the remaining balance can build interest quickly. Credit card interest rates can be high, so a small unpaid balance can grow over time.

Late payment fees may apply when you miss a payment. These can add up quickly if your balance keeps growing.

shopping_bag BNPL: Afterpay, Zip & More

Buy-now-pay-later services let you split purchases into smaller payments. They can feel easier than paying upfront, but missed payments can lead to late fees.

Multiple active BNPL payments can become hard to track, especially if your income changes week to week.

warning Hidden Conditions to Watch

  • Interest-free periods can end and interest may apply.
  • Minimum repayments reduce your balance slowly.
  • Store credit cards may have higher interest rates.
  • Some BNPL providers may charge account or late fees.
  • Too many small payments can become hard to manage.

Tax & Super Basics

Learn how payslips, PAYG tax, tax returns, and superannuation work.

Explore

When you start working, understanding your payslip, tax, and superannuation can help you avoid stress and plan better.

description Your Payslip Explained

A sample payslip for someone earning $750 per week:

Sample Payslip — Weekly
Gross Income Before tax $750.00
Tax Withheld Deduction −$97.00
Superannuation Your future $86.25
Net Take-Home Pay $653.00

Super is paid by your employer into your super fund. It does not go directly into your bank account.

assignment_return Tax Return Tips

Each year, the ATO checks whether you paid too much or too little tax. If you overpaid, you may receive a refund.

  • Keep records of work-related expenses.
  • Check if your study costs relate to your current job.
  • Keep receipts for uniforms or protective gear.
  • Review phone or internet costs used for work.

savings Understanding Superannuation

Super is money set aside for retirement. Your employer usually contributes a percentage of your earnings into your chosen super fund.

It is helpful to check whether you have more than one super account, because multiple accounts can mean multiple fees.

Victorian Tenant Rights

Understand bonds, repairs, rent increases, and rental rights in Victoria.

Explore

Renting for the first time? Understanding your rights as a tenant in Victoria can protect you from unfair treatment and unexpected costs.

gavel Key Rights Before You Sign

  • You should receive a copy of the rental agreement.
  • Bond is usually lodged with the RTBA.
  • You should receive a condition report.
  • Rent increases must follow notice rules.
  • Take photos when you move in and move out.

construction Repairs & Maintenance

Your rental provider is responsible for keeping the property in good repair. Urgent repairs should be addressed quickly.

attach_money Getting Your Bond Back

Bond can only be claimed for genuine issues beyond fair wear and tear. Photos and condition reports can help protect you.
