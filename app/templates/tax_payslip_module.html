Gross income

The amount earned before taxes and deductions are applied. This may include base pay, overtime, tips, bonuses, or allowances.

Tax withheld (PAYG)

The amount of tax your employer deducts from your pay and sends to the Australian Taxation Office on your behalf.

Superannuation contribution

Money paid into your super fund for retirement. This is generally paid by your employer in addition to your regular wages.

Net take-home pay

The amount that reaches your bank account after tax and deductions have been taken out.

Tax Return Tips

A tax refund happens when you have paid more tax during the financial year than you needed to. After lodging your tax return, the government may return some of that money to you.

If you earned income during the financial year, you may need to lodge a tax return.

Common Deductibles

Work Clothing & Uniforms

  • Protective clothing, such as steel-cap boots
  • Occupation-specific uniforms
  • Laundry costs for eligible uniforms

Travel & Transport

  • Work-related car or public transport travel
  • Parking fees and tolls for work trips
  • Regular travel between home and work is usually not deductible.

Tools & Equipment

  • Laptops, tablets, and phones used for work
  • Work-related software
  • Tools and equipment needed for your job

Working From Home Expenses

  • Internet usage
  • Electricity
  • Home office equipment
  • Phone expenses

Self-Education & Study

  • Courses related to your current work
  • Textbooks and study materials
  • Student union or course fees, where eligible
