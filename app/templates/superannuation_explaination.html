Q1: What super is and why does it matter?

savings

Super is money for future you

Right now, your pay helps with everyday life: rent, food, transport, study, bills, and going out. But when you work, your employer also puts extra money into a super fund for later in life.

trending_up

It grows over time

You usually cannot spend super now, but it is still your money. It is saved and invested so it can grow over time. Think of it as a financial safety fund for the older version of you.

Q2: How much is the employer contribution rate?

percent

The contribution rate is 12%

If you are working in Australia, your employer usually has to pay extra money into your super fund. This is called the Superannuation Guarantee. From 1 July 2025, the employer contribution rate is 12% of your ordinary time earnings.

payments

Example: $500 earned means about $60 to super

If you earn $500 in a week, your employer would generally need to pay about $60 into your super fund. That $60 does not go into your everyday bank account. It goes into your super account for future you.

Q3: What can I do with my super?

account_balance

Choose a super fund

Your super fund is where your super money is kept and invested. Different funds can have different fees, investment options, and performance. Choosing a good fund can help your money grow over time.

monitoring

Check how your super is invested

Your super is usually invested in things like shares, property, and other assets. You may see options like conservative, balanced, or high growth.

manage_search

Keep track of your super

If you work different casual or part-time jobs, you might end up with more than one super account. Multiple accounts can mean multiple sets of fees.

add_card

Add extra money if it suits you

You can add extra money to your super from your own savings or through your pay. This is optional, but small contributions can grow over time.
